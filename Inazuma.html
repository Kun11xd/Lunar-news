Ritou

During the Sakoku Decree, you had to pass through the checkpoint in Ritou before reaching the Narukami's residence.

Ritou was under the jurisdiction of the Kanjou Commission, and people needed various types of travel permits to enter and exit. But to obtain these permits, you had to go through a very complicated procedure and have some special connections.

Without these permits, you could only stay in Ritou for a short time, alongside other foreigners, gazing at the red maples atop the green roof tiles of this remote fishing village.

Inazuma City

Inazuma's most bustling and prosperous region, home to the majority of Inazuma's population.

From Hanamizaka to the city streets, you can continue along the trails to visit traditional local shops and sample Inazuma's signature dishes.

The Tenryou Commission is also located in the city, overseeing matters of public order and security, as well as the Shogunate's weaponry. At the highest point in Inazuma City stands Tenshukaku. This is where the Raiden Shogun resides and rules Inazuma with his iron fist.

Chinju Forest

A serene and mysterious forest nestled at the foot of Mount Yougou.

Tanuki statues can be found everywhere, torii gates scattered throughout the forest, and abandoned shrines hidden deep within... It tells a legend that flows with time, like the flow of a river.

Rumor has it that if you walk in Chinju Forest, you may be haunted by mysterious creatures...

Grand Narukami Shrine

Inazuma's largest shrine, located atop Mount Yougou, is dedicated to His Holiness, the Almighty Narukami Ogosho.

Those with a strong will can climb the steps to the summit, step by step. Those blessed by Electro can use the Thunder Sphere to soar upwards and reach the giant Sacred Sakura tree that surrounds the entire shrine. The Sacred Sakura blossoms and never fades, just like Narukami, who is eternal and everlasting.

After passing through the numerous torii gates, meet the shrine maiden to have your fortune told and ask for Narukami's blessings and protection.

Tatarasuna, Kannazuka

Inazuma's largest shrine, located atop Mount Yougou, is dedicated to His Holiness, the Almighty Narukami Ogosho.

Kannazuka's terrain is steep, with towering rock walls. Among these layers of rock stands Inazuma's largest smelting facility, the Mikage Furnace.

This is where Jade Steel, a rare material used to forge swords in Inazuma, is produced.

Kujou Encampment

The Shogunate's military encampment in Kannazuka. There has always been a perpetual conflict between the Narukami people, who believe in the Raiden Shogun, and the Sangonomiya people, who believe in Watatsumi Omikami.

This conflict has intensified since the enactment of the Vision Hunting Decree. The Tenryou Commission stationed Shogunate forces here to fight the Sangonomiya rebels.

Compared to the Sangonomiya forces, the Shogunate forces possessed greater numbers, more advanced weaponry, and a more integrated system. All of this seemed to signal that victory was within their grasp.

Musoujin Gorge

This narrow and majestic rift valley traverses the eastern side of Yashiori Island.

This magnificent sight was not created naturally, but rather created when the Electro Archon slashed the giant Snake God. This rift valley is called "Musou no Hitotachi," a name derived from the Electro Archon's most powerful art.

To this day, the remains of the giant snake's body are still scattered across Yashiori Island. The Tatarigami that emerged from this god's remains still faintly move about.

Serpent's Head

The giant snake god, slain by Musou no Hitotachi, the Electro Archon, now remains only its desiccated corpse. Though its head still tilts toward the sky, the god is no longer alive, and the name Watatsumi Omikami is no longer heard aloud.

When gods die, they leave behind a powerful energy. Due to the effects of the Tatarigami, Yashiori Island is uninhabitable.

Now, only torrential rain and lightning wash away the remnants of the past

Nazuchi Beach

Nazuchi Beach, located between Yashiori Island and Kannazuka Island, is a battle-torn place. On the shallow shoreline, you can find fallen purple ibises, and traces of battle, such as torn flags and broken arrows.

There's also the remains of sunken ships, such as the remains of sails and broken deck planks.

Due to the impact of the war, this place was often visited by pirates and ronin seeking to plunder the spoils of war. Even rare Dendrobiums bloom here...

Face endless thunderstorms, and set foot on an island covered in red maples and cherry blossoms. On winding coastlines, on towering cliffs, in forests and mountains filled with secrets, witness Immortality pursued by His Majesty — the Almighty Narukami Ogosho.
