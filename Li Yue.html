Mt. Tianheng

The greatest pride of the mountain range surrounding the western part of Liyue Harbor, this mountain also served as a formidable barrier against enemy attacks from the west.

Numerous war records, both large and small, record this area as a defensive point against a dominant enemy force. Therefore, this area is often seen with war relics, including the ruins of city walls, fortifications, and defensive structures.

This mountain was also the first place where Liyue's mining operations began.

Harbor

Since the port's founding, Liyue's maritime trade has boomed, and its bustling atmosphere has never waned.

Already the largest trading port in Teyvat, it boasts a volume of goods flowing in and out of the port, unmatched by any other.

It also offers a sight not to be missed every year during the Lantern Ritual festival, where thousands of lanterns soar high into the darkness, illuminating the night sky.

Dihua Marsh

Dihua Marsh, a valley area located north of Liyue, takes its name from the silvergrass (called "dihua" in the local language) commonly found in shallow marshes.

This point where rivers meet has naturally formed a unique ecosystem rich in flora and fauna, providing a stunning view for adventurers who visit. It also serves as a road between Mondstadt and Liyue, and serves as a resting place for merchants.

In the center of this area stands a renowned inn called the Wangshu Inn, which also serves as a landmark for travelers heading to Mondstadt or Liyue.

Wangshu Inn

A landmark building within Dihua Marsh, standing tall atop a massive stone pillar. Most of the travelers who pass through here are merchants, so the Wangshu Inn has been purposely provided with a space and stalls to facilitate their leisure time.

More than just a simple inn, from the highest point of the Wangshu Inn, visitors can also enjoy a view that never fails to amaze first-time visitors.

On clear days, the Inn offers a breathtaking view of Mt. Qingce and Jueyunjian. Rumors circulate within the trading community that a mysterious secret lies hidden within the inn, but no one has yet uncovered its true meaning.

Qingce Village

In the northernmost part of Liyue, amidst a towering bamboo forest, lies Qingce Village.

The view of rolling white clouds hiding the mountain peaks is enhanced by the crescent-shaped layers of terraced rice paddies.

When the wind blows, the water's surface dances, reflecting beautiful rainbows.

Jueyunjian

A lofty mountain peak hidden within the mist and clouds of northwest Liyue. The summit offers breathtaking views, but reaching it requires a rigorous, intricate path. It is also said to be the abode of the adepti, who protect Liyue, and many believers attempt to climb it.

However, for those less experienced in wilderness survival, the view is best enjoyed from the distant windows of the Wangshu Inn.
